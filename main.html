About

Academic background and current position

Prof. John Doe is a Professor of Power Electronics at the Department of Electrical Engineering, University of Example. His research focuses on the modeling, design, and control of power electronic converters for renewable energy integration, high-performance motor drives, and industrial automation.


He received his Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering in 2012 and has since coordinated several research projects in collaboration with industry partners and international institutions.

Key interests: high-efficiency conversion, reliability, and application-oriented control for real-world energy systems.
  • Education: B.Sc., M.Sc., and Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering.
  • Affiliations: IEEE Power Electronics Society, IEEE Industrial Electronics Society.
  • Editorial roles: Reviewer for IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics, IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics, and related journals.

Research Areas

Main topics and technical focus

My research agenda is centered on the development of efficient, reliable, and intelligent power conversion systems that enable large-scale penetration of renewable energy and electrification of transportation and industry.

Multilevel converters & modular topologies Grid-tied PV & wind energy systems Motor drives & electrified transportation Wide-bandgap devices (SiC/GaN) Model predictive & digital control EMI, reliability & fault tolerance Real-time HIL & experimental validation
